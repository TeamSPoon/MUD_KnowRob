EYE and OWL 2

EYE - Euler Yet another proof Engine

EYE is an inference engine supporting logic based proofs. It is a backward-forward-backward chaining reasoner enhanced with Euler path detection.
The backward-forward-backward chaining is realized via an underlying Prolog backward chaining, a forward meta-level reasoning and a backward proof construction.

EYE is looking throuh N3 glasses and doing inferencing in N3Logic. Internally EYE is using PCL (Prolog Coherent Logic) intermediate code. Every FOL (First Order Logic) theory has a conservative extension that is equivalent to a PCL theory, a result that goes back to Thoralf Skolem who developed Coherent Logic to obtain metamathematical results in lattice theory and projective geometry [1][2].

EYE strongly benefits from the demand driven indexing of YAP-Prolog and when EYE is also driven by demand driven theory selection, the performance issues can be engineered in a scalable way. For instance a case with 300000 PCL subsumption clauses takes EYE 4 sec whereas it takes EyeS (same engine on top of SWI-Prolog) 10 hours and EyeJ (same engine on top of JLog) 68 hours.
